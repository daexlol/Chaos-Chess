📖 How to Play Chaos Chess

The Basics

Chaos Chess is standard chess with a twist: after every N moves (default 10), one of the players drafts a modifier — a rule-bending power-up that changes how pieces move, interact, or explode.

Modifiers & Drafting

Every N moves (default 10), a player (alternating each N moves) is presented with 3 random modifiers to choose from. Pick wisely — your choice is permanent for the rest of the game.

⬜ Common
💜 Rare
🟡 Legendary
✨ Mythic

Higher rarity modifiers are rarer but more powerful. Mythic modifiers can completely reshape the game.

Example Modifiers

Kamikaze Pawn Captured pawns explode after 1 turn, destroying any piece on the armed square.
Phantom Knight Knights become untargetable for 1 turn after capturing
Jester Knight Knights spawn a fake decoy knight on a random legal move square.
Tornado de Caballos Place a tornado which pulls in nearby enemy pieces for 3 turns. Your pieces are safe.

Modifier Evolutions

Some modifiers can evolve into a more powerful version. If you own a modifier that has an evolution, there's a chance it appears as an option in a future draft. Picking it replaces the base modifier with the evolved version — so it's always an upgrade.

Evolution cards are marked with a special ✦ EVOLVED label and a rainbow animated border in the draft. Base modifiers that can evolve show a ✧ CAN EVOLVE hint in the draft and in their info panel. Once evolved, the hint changes to ✦ EVOLVED.

✦ Evolved

Evolutions are intentionally rare — each draft slot only has a small chance of becoming an evolution offer, even when you own an evolvable modifier.

Online Play

Sign in to play rated games and track your Chaos Rating. You can also play private games with friends using a room code.

Local Play

Pass & Play on the same device. No account needed. Great for learning the game.

Tips

• Click on any modifier badge during a game to read its full description and a sneaky lore snapshot.
• High-rarity modifiers stack — the more powerful your combo, the more unpredictable the game.
• Right-click squares on the board to draw arrows and highlight squares for analysis.
